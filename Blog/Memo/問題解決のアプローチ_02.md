# 問題解決のアプローチ（2）

## 自分を疑う Self-Doubt

説明を受けても聞いていなかったなんてことはないだろうか。
知っていることだとかまえて聞くのはあまりに慢心。初めて聞くことかのように聞く。
質問はありませんか、と言われて質問がないのは話を主体的に聞いていなかった可能性が高い。
すぐに質問が思いつかないのであれば、聞きたいことは最初からメモを用意しておくなり準備をして挑むなどの対策が必要。
時間は有限なのだからできるだけ効率がいい方法で一つ一つ解決していく。
日々主体的に聞こうとする姿勢を持とう。それは仕事中だけでなく日常生活でも同じことだ。

When receiving explanations, it's important to consider whether you've truly absorbed the information. Assuming you already know something can be a sign of arrogance. Approach each explanation as if it's the first time you're hearing it. If prompted with "Any questions?", the absence of inquiries could indicate passive listening. If questions don't come to mind immediately, it's advisable to prepare by jotting down queries beforehand.

Time is limited, so it's important to manage tasks efficiently and ensure they are completed in a timely manner. This involves task management, setting deadlines, and completing tasks that need to be done. Setting deadlines helps prioritize tasks and improves efficiency in completing them, ensuring they are addressed appropriately.

Furthermore, it's prudent not to accept information blindly. For instance, when given a bandage at a hospital, don't simply assume it's enough to apply it. Consider practicalities: How should it be applied? What if it comes off? How long should it be kept on?
